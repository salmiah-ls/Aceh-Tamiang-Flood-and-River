## Aceh Tamiang: TC Senyar Flood Impact & Hydrological Baseline
### Created with Google Earth Engine (GEE)

- The first dashboard captures the scale of inundation following Tropical Cyclone Senyar. By analyzing Sentinel-1 VH SAR backscatter shifts between October and December 2025, approximately 3,149 hectares of flooded land were identified (shown in black). To assess humanitarian risk, the flood extent was intersected with critical infrastructure data obtained via the Google Places API. The spatial overlap with health facilities (red) and education centers (blue) identifies high-vulnerability zones where essential services faced the greatest disruption. <br />
Link: https://secret-well-475403-j1.projects.earthengine.app/view/aceh-tamiang-flood

- The second dashboard shows a permanent water baseline by integrating HydroSHEDS and JRC Global Surface Water datasets. By mapping frequent river networks and natural drainage patterns, the map provides the necessary hydrological context to distinguish between seasonal water bodies and temporary inundation. This foundational data serves as a critical tool for long-term water management and urban resilience planning in flood-prone areas. <br />
Link: https://secret-well-475403-j1.projects.earthengine.app/view/aceh-tamiang-river
